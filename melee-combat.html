Melee Combat System

Responsive Unreal Engine 5 combat system with AI-driven behavior

This project is a melee combat prototype developed in Unreal Engine 5, focused on responsive sword-based gameplay, enemy interaction, and readable combat feedback. The system combines player attacks, hit detection, damage response, and AI behavior into a structured gameplay loop designed to feel reactive and polished.

System Overview

Combat Prototype Breakdown

The prototype was built in Unreal Engine 5 using Blueprints as the primary gameplay scripting layer, allowing the combat loop to be structured around readable event-driven logic. Player attacks, damage application, reactions, and state updates are all coordinated through interconnected systems that prioritize responsiveness and clean handoff between actions. Rather than treating combat as isolated animations, the project was designed as a full gameplay pipeline where hit results, enemy reactions, and follow-up logic feed into one another with clear timing.

On the AI side, Behavior Trees and Blackboards were used to organize enemy decision-making into readable states such as patrol, detect, chase, and engage. This created a solid foundation for reactive enemies that transition naturally from ambient navigation into combat behavior. The use of structured Blackboard data also helped keep awareness, targeting, and combat conditions synchronized across the AI flow.

Animation-driven attack logic was an important part of making the system feel convincing. Rather than relying on loose timing alone, attacks are tied to animation events so traces, damage windows, and combat responses happen at intentional moments. That approach improved combat readability, made feedback more consistent, and reinforced clean state transitions between idle, pursuit, attack, reaction, and recovery.

Core Interaction

Gameplay Systems

The gameplay layer was built around a readable attack flow that starts with animation timing and resolves through hit detection, damage application, and enemy response. Combat windows are controlled so traces only activate during the intended swing moments, which helps attacks feel deliberate rather than noisy or inconsistent.

Hit detection is designed to register contact clearly and consistently, feeding into damage response and combat feedback systems that make each strike understandable to the player. Health updates, reaction logic, and impact handling were structured to support readability so players can quickly understand when an attack connected and how the enemy reacted.

Responsiveness was a major priority throughout the prototype. The result is a combat loop that feels immediate while still remaining legible, with clear transitions between attacking, confirming hits, and returning to the next gameplay decision.

Decision Flow

Enemy AI Behavior

Enemy behavior was structured around a clear patrol to detect to chase to attack flow, giving encounters a readable escalation from passive movement into direct engagement. This helped the combat prototype feel more like a playable gameplay loop rather than a collection of isolated mechanics.

Behavior Trees were used to organize branching decisions, while Blackboards stored the key context that AI needed to react to the player, manage targeting, and shift between combat states. This kept the system modular and made it easier to tune enemy responses without collapsing the whole logic chain into one large graph.

Clean state transitions were central to the design. Enemies move from patrol into awareness, then into pursuit and attack, while combat reactions feed back into those states in a controlled way so the flow remains stable, readable, and expandable.
